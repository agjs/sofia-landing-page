Artificial Intelligence

The good, the bad and the ugly

Intelligent software is transforming our world from behind the scenes, and jumping into the limelight by directly interacting with us. Nevertheless, it remains to be seen if the automation revolution will actually improve the lives of most people.

While AI is beneficial to many aspects of economic activity, some are sounding the alarm for the global employment picture, along with more dire scenarios, and artificial stupidity adding to the natural kind!

Without falling for the hype, we cannot ignore the warning signs: many jobs are being replaced and not just displaced, in part because the demand in new cognitive skills, is changing faster than standard education can adapt to. New learning tools are needed fill this gap.

A new niche of skills is emerging as significant: those of strong conceptual thinkers. AI is a set of technologies more or less adapted to perform specific tasks incredibly well, but conceptualizing for the human experience is not its strong point! AI lacks critical fundamental traits: genuine understanding, insight and consciousness. The mind transcends computation, at least in the sense of Church-Turing.

Meta-skills helps us monitor our own thought patterns, and correct our own biases, illusions and fallacies, while strengthening the awareness our genuine mental goals. We define Meta-skills as (1) portable (not attached to a job), (2) universal (applicable to any cognitive challenge) and (3) critical (essential to modern economic activity: STEM, Biz, Finance etc).


These meta-skills are the ones Corporations say they need most, but cannot find.

SOFIA is a company who's vision is to harness the strengths of AI to liberate us from mindless tasks, by boosting our own meta- skills; to help us become high-level conceptual thinkers; to help us create new imaginative goals, instead of passively being replaced by automation. A jump in our capacity to conceptualize, and meta-cognitive skills, will help us morph the basic concept of worthwhile endeavors.

CLX Executive Summary May 5, 2015

We live in an era where education and funding systems encourage me-too, GroupThink, empire-building, safe low-risk/low-reward thinking, while intelligent software and automation are replacing both blue and white collar jobs at an accelerating pace. In a nutshell, this standard approach makes the work space harder each day, for both employers and employees. This alarming trend indicates that we should instead be encouraging transformational high-risk/high-reward original thinking, which generates new kinds of original, non-existent opportunities, work and careers. The Cognitive Landscape eXplorer (CLX) is a game enabling users to become comfortable and skilled at transformational high-risk/high-reward and cutting-edge original thinking. CLX is a cloud-based exploration game played under the guidance of an “exploration mentor” (an artificial intelligence driven avatar). A set of fundamental cognitive challenges are “explorations” the user performs, to boost the “meta-skills” needed for high-level creative thinking. The meta-skills are measurable, because these conceptual challenges are at the basis of STEM ( Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics ). During the exploration of a fundamental cognitive challenge, CLX guides the explorer at two levels: cognitive and meta-cognitive. CLX provides the user this dual guidance system while reasoning about a challenge. Metacognition guides cognition, cognition guides exploration, exploration informs metacognition: a self-regulating feedback loop, which organizes the otherwise free thinking process during exploration. Making a habit of thinking using CLX’s dual guidance enhances the “meta-skills” critical for leadership, strategic thinking, and original problem-solving; the skills corporations say they need most, but cannot find [Bloomberg, 2015].

THE METACOGNITION ENGINE April 1, 2015

Mathematical Model of a Metacognition Engine for Exploring Fundamental Conceptual Challenges Edouard Siregar Founder, SOFIA SOFIA Internal Communication Journal – Copyright 2015 _____________________________________________________ Abstract The Landscape Explorer (LEX) is a cloud-based individual trainer to help users become strong original thinkers: o strong high-level conceptual thinkers o strong meta-cognitive problem-solvers o consistent integrators of multiple reasoning modes o keystone hub connectors of seemingly unrelated distant ideas These unique and irreplaceable “meta-skills” enable people to design unconventional career paths and jobs, in a global economy where intelligent software and robotic automation are replacing low conceptualization jobs at an accelerating pace. Learning is achieved by guided exploration of fundamental cognitive challenges mostly from STEM (Science Technology Engineering Mathematics). Meta-cognition guides reasoning by monitoring, verifying and justifying ideas and actions. LEX guides the exploration of a cognitive challenge, by using its Meta-cognition Engine, which gives explorers a situation awareness of where they are in their own mental space. The meta-cognition engine is interpreted mathematically as a finite graph embedded in a 3D space,implemented algorithmically as a Finite State Machine (FSM). A mental space S composed of mindsets {M}, provides the framework for guiding exploration and learning of thinking skills. Each mindset M in S, gives the user meta-cognitive guidance during the exploration of a challenge (a curve C, generated by the time-evolution of the mindset M, in mental space S). In short, LEX guides the exploration of cognitive challenges, by suggesting a clear mindset to adopt at each instant of exploration. Strong meta-cognitive thinking is a capacity to select & exploit suitable mindsets, given a cognitive challenge at hand.
